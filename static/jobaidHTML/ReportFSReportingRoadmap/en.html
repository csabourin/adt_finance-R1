This is a flowchart depicting how a Financial Situation Report is prepared and used.

To start the process, Finance sends out a call letter to the manager and/or their administrative staff asking them to complete the Financial Situation Report. The manager asks their administrative staff to pull detailed information on their budget, commitments and actual expenditures from the financial system. The manager validates that the information is accurate. Then the manager and/or the administrative staff update commitments and actuals in the financial system. The manager provides forecasts to inform their surplus or deficit, explains reasons for any surplus or deficit, indicates how any deficit will be managed, and identifies risks that could impact forecasts.

The manager then submits the Financial Situation Report to Finance. Finance consolidates all of the managers’ information for the Assistant Deputy Minister. A Financial Management Advisor presents it to the Assistant Deputy Minister and makes the requested adjustments.

Finance consolidates all of the ADMs’ information for the Deputy Minister and the Executive Committee and presents it to them. The Deputy Minister and the Executive Committee strategize and make decisions to influence year-end results.
