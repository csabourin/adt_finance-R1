This job aid provides an overview of how the priorities of the Government of Canada are established and describes how these choices cascade down to influence departmental plans and work activities.

The process starts with the Speech from the Throne, which outlines the government’s priorities. After the Speech from the Throne, the Prime Minister addresses a mandate letter to each minister. These mandate letters influence the Departmental Results Framework of each organization, which in turn influences their Departmental Plans and thus the priorities of their sector of operations, branches and directorates.

Managers then propose their work plans and activities for the fiscal year to align with these priorities. They conduct a 360-degree scan, reviewing last year’s work plan, budgets and reports to identify the activities needed for the next fiscal year. They then update their work plans in consideration of these activities and submit them for approval. If changes are required to their work plans, they will be returned to the manager for updating and then resubmitted until final approval is received.
